## üåè What is Networking? (picture)
Networking refers to the process of connecting computers, devices, or people so they can communicate, share information, and access resources.
It can mean:

Computer networking ‚Äî devices connected by the internet/Wi-Fi to exchange data.

- Human networking ‚Äî building professional or personal relationships.

- Most of the time in Information Systems, networking = computer/technology networks.

In short:

#### Networking is connecting systems and people so information can flow efficiently and securely.

## ‚≠êHow it apply in real world?

**1. Internet & Communication**

Email, social media (Instagram, TikTok, LINE), Zoom, Teams‚Äîall work because of global computer networks.

People can communicate instantly, anywhere in the world.

**2. Business Operations**

Companies rely on networks to coordinate everything:

Databases (ERP, CRM)

Cloud storage (Google Drive, OneDrive)

Payment systems (POS, online transactions)

**3. Smart Homes & IoT**

Networking connects smart devices so they work together:

Smart door locks

Google Home / Alexa

Smart AC and lighting

Security cameras

**4. Transportation & Navigation**

Networking powers real-time systems:

Google Maps traffic updates

Uber/Grab location tracking

Airport flight operation systems

Metro card systems (Suica, Octopus, EasyCard)

**5. Finance & Banking**

Networking enables global money movement:

ATM withdrawals

Mobile payments (PayPay, Line Pay, Apple Pay)

International transfers (SWIFT)

Stock trading systems
